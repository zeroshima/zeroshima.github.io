Do what matters most

Successful Transformation

Successful Transformation shows how an individual can respond and adapt to change. Questions that test this behaviour include the following:

  1. Describe a major change that occurred in a job that you held. How did you adapt to this change?
  2. Tell us about a situation in which you had to adjust to changes over which you had no control. How did you handle it?
  3. Tell us about a time that you had to adapt to a difficult situation.
  4. What do you do when priorities change quickly? Give one example of when this happened.
  5. Describe a time when you were faced with a stressful situation?
  6. Tell me about a time when you received negative feedback from your manager. How did you handle this?
  7. Tell me about a time you had to quickly adjust your work priorities to meet changing demands.
  8. Tell me about a time when you had to meet scheduled deadlines and your work was being continually disrupted? What was the most difficult challenge? how did you overcome it?
  9. Can you tell me about a situation in which you had to change your initial approach to a project or assignment in order to achieve your goal?

Your behavioral example should show:

  • You maintain performance and your effectiveness in different situations and faced with different people
  • Include how you are able to adjust your approach to match different demands and challenges

Recognized Adaptability strategies to focus on in your behavioral example include:

  • Able to perform under pressure and cope with difficult situations
  • Show that you can stay calm and you are able to plan to reduce stress.
  • Able to sort out issues immediately
  • Anticipate likely problems
  • Ability to handle disappointment and rejection
  • Show how you handle criticism whilst maintaining enthusiasm and performance
  • Show you can effectively perform in different environments and with different tasks
  • Show that you can adjust your actions to meet changing demands and tasks.

Inspirational Communicator

Inspirational Communicator shows how an individual can adapt communication styles and convey their message. Questions that test this behaviour include the following:

Example Questions

  1. Describe a situation when you were able to strengthen a relationship by communicating effectively. What made your communication effective?
  2. Describe a situation where you felt you had not communicated well. How did you correct the situation?
  3. Have you ever had to “sell” an idea to your co‐workers or group? How did you do it? Did they “buy” it?
  4. How do you go about explaining a complex technical problem to a person who does not understand technical jargon?
  5. What approach do you take in communicating with people?
  6. Tell us about a time when you had to use your verbal communication skills in order to get a point across that was important to you?
  7. Tell us about a recent successful experience in making a speech or presentation. How did you prepare? What obstacles did you face? How did you handle them?
  8. Describe a time you had to ask questions and listen carefully to clarify the exact nature of a customer's/colleague's concern?
  9. How have you responded to negative feedback?
  10. Tell me about a time you had to talk to an angry customer. What steps did you take?
  11. Describe a situation when you were able to successfully influence another person?
  12. How do you influence clients? Can you give me an example?
  13. Tell us about an experience in which you had to speak up in order to be sure that other people knew what you thought or felt.

Behavioral interview answers to questions that explore your ability to persuade and influence others should highlight how you select the right style of communication and interaction for the situation and individual you are dealing with.


Your behavioral example should show:

  • How you identify the individual or group's needs
  • How you persuade them you can satisfy these needs with your product, service, approach or idea

Your answer should close with proof of how you gained commitment to the action you persuaded them to take.


Recognized Persuasive/Influence strategy to focus on in your behavioral example include:

  • Active listening
  • Focusing on the positive
  • Establishing common ground
  • Demonstrating expertise and credibility
  • Communicating in a calm but assertive manner
  • Handling objections

Good examples of Persuasiveness and Influencing others in the workplace include:

  • Getting people to accept change
  • Getting a project approved
  • Making a difficult sale
  • Getting an idea or recommendation accepted
  • Changing an individual's behavior or actions
  • Convincing someone to see things your way
  • Reaching agreement
  • Negotiating a solution to a problem
  • Obtaining resources (budget, skills) to help you perform
  • Resolving disputes
  • Motivating a team or individual towards goal achievement
  • Negotiating a change to conditions of employment such as salary or working hours
  • Getting a change to your job description approved
