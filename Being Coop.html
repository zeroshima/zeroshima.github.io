Four Ways of Being Coop

The four Ways of Being Co-op guide our future – no matter what we do, they’re how we do it.

They are:

I - Do what matters most

  • Vision & Belief
  • Future Focused
  • Successful Transformation(1)

II - Be yourself, always

  • Inspirational Communicator(2)
  • Personal Growth(3)
  • Endless Inclusion

III - Show you care

  • Forging Relationships(4)
  • Championing Coop
  • Developing Others

IV - Succeed together

  • Co-operation
  • Driving Innovation
  • Speaking Up

Do what matters most

1. Successful Transformation

Successful Transformation shows how an individual can respond and adapt to change. Questions that test this behaviour include the following:

Example Questions

  1. Describe a major change that occurred in a job that you held. How did you adapt to this change?
  2. Tell us about a time that you had to adapt to a difficult situation.
  3. What do you do when priorities change quickly? Give one example of when this happened.
  4. Tell me about a time when you received negative feedback from your manager. How did you handle this?
  5. Tell me about a time you had to quickly adjust your work priorities to meet changing demands.
  6. Can you tell me about a situation in which you had to change your initial approach to a project or assignment in order to achieve your goal?

Recognized Adaptability strategies to focus on in your behavioral example include:

  • Able to perform under pressure and cope with difficult situations
  • Demonstrate that you can adjust your approach
  • Show that you can stay calm and you are able to plan to reduce stress.
  • Able to sort out issues immediately
  • Anticipate likely problems
  • Ability to handle disappointment and rejection
  • Show how you handle criticism whilst maintaining enthusiasm and performance
  • Show you can effectively perform in different environments and with different tasks
  • Show that you can adjust your actions to meet changing demands and tasks.
  • Be confident
  • Be open-minded
  • Embrace the unknown

Good examples of Adaptability in the workplace include:

  • Accepting change
  • Major changes you had to deal with
  • Difficult situations
  • Negative feedback
  • Changing priorities
  • New tasks
  • Differing working styles of colleagues
  • Putting in extra hours due to sudden changes
  • New ways of working
  • New systems

Be yourself, always

2. Inspirational Communicator

Inspirational Communicator shows how an individual can adapt communication styles and convey their message. Questions that test this behaviour include the following:

Example Questions

  1. Describe a situation when you were able to strengthen a relationship by communicating effectively. What made your communication effective?
  2. Describe a situation where you felt you had not communicated well. How did you correct the situation?
  3. Have you ever had to “sell” an idea to your co‐workers or group? How did you do it? Did they “buy” it?
  4. How do you go about explaining a complex technical problem to a person who does not understand technical jargon?
  5. What approach do you take in communicating with people?
  6. Tell us about a time when you had to use your verbal communication skills in order to get a point across that was important to you?
  7. Tell us about a recent successful experience in making a speech or presentation. How did you prepare? What obstacles did you face? How did you handle them?
  8. Describe a time you had to ask questions and listen carefully to clarify the exact nature of a customer's/colleague's concern?
  9. How have you responded to negative feedback?
  10. Tell me about a time you had to talk to an angry customer. What steps did you take?
  11. Describe a situation when you were able to successfully influence another person?
  12. How do you influence clients? Can you give me an example?
  13. Tell us about an experience in which you had to speak up in order to be sure that other people knew what you thought or felt.

Behavioral interview answers to questions that explore your ability to persuade and influence others should highlight how you select the right style of communication and interaction for the situation and individual you are dealing with.

Your answer should close with proof of how you gained commitment to the action you persuaded them to take.


Recognized Persuasive/Influence strategy to focus on in your behavioral example include:

  • Active listening
  • Focusing on the positive
  • Establishing common ground
  • Demonstrating expertise and credibility
  • Communicating in a calm but assertive manner
  • Handling objections
  • How you identify the individual or group's needs
  • How you persuade them you can satisfy these needs with your product, service, approach or idea

Good examples of Persuasiveness and Influencing others in the workplace include:

  • Getting people to accept change
  • Getting a project approved
  • Making a difficult sale
  • Getting an idea or recommendation accepted
  • Changing an individual's behavior or actions
  • Convincing someone to see things your way
  • Reaching agreement
  • Negotiating a solution to a problem
  • Obtaining resources (budget, skills) to help you perform
  • Resolving disputes
  • Motivating a team or individual towards goal achievement
  • Negotiating a change to conditions of employment such as salary or working hours
  • Getting a change to your job description approved

Be yourself, always

3. Personal Growth

Personal growth shows how you focus on personal development within Co-op; developing skills and capabilities to benefit me, my team and wider community. Questions that test this behaviour include the following:

Example Questions

  1. What have you done to improve yourself in the past year?
  2. Have you undertaken any areas of self-improvement in your career?
  3. Tell me about areas where you need to improve and what you have done to address these areas?
  4. Describe your own strengths and areas for improvement?
  5. How do you assess your own ability and knowledge relative to your role?
  6. Give an example of when you expanded your role with a new challenge?

Recognized Personal Growth strategies to focus on in your behavioral example include:

  • Using all situations as potential learning opportunities
  • Being a role model by devoting time to own development
  • Engaging in formal and informal learning and development activities
  • Updating professional/ specialist skillsEngaging positively with appraisal processes
  • Seeking, accepting and acting upon feedback, and learning from it

Good examples of Personal Growth in the workplace include:

  • Changes you have made to improve in your previous role.
  • Constructive criticism you may have received.
  • Learning new things.
  • Learning from past mistakes.
  • Demonstrate attitude to continuous learning.
  • Examples of growth mindset.
  • What goals have you set, acheived or missed.
  • Maintaining positive attitude.
  • Experiences you have learned from.
  • Taking responsibility for mistakes.
  • Discovered problems in your own performance, and addressed them.

Show you Care

4. Forging Relationships

Take action to build and maintain trusted relationships, in order to understand and support my colleagues. Think about how you have built meaningful relationships with colleagues. Questions that test this behaviour include the following:

  1. Describe a time that you demonstrated the ability to be an effective team member.
  2. Describe a team experience you found dissapointing.
  3. Describe how you worked in a team to to accomplish an important task.
  4. Tell us about a time you had to work as part of a team that didnt get along.
  5. What role have you typically played as a team member? How did you interact with the other team members?
  6. Tell us about a time when you worked with someone and didnt share the same ideas? How did you acheve the objective?

These questions are looking at your ability to build relationships and contribute to team success.

Recognized Teamwork strategies to focus on in your behavioral example include:

  • Communicate and work well with others
  • Contribute to the team goal
  • Build relationships
  • Motivate team members to do better
  • Bring out the best in people
  • Learn from your mistakes and produce positive outcomes.

Good examples of Teamwork in the workplace include:

  • Connecting with new team member
  • Learning opportunities
  • Conflict with team members
